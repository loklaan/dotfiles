# Split — Breaking Up Long-Running Tickets

When a ticket runs long — especially once it spans **multiple sprints** — stop
and split it. A ticket that never closes hides its own progress, dodges
re-prioritisation, and rots its estimate. Splitting converts an open-ended slog
into a closed deliverable plus a fresh, re-decidable chunk.

## The move

1. **Stop and write the outcome so far.** Close the current ticket with a
   tap-out note covering what's *actually been delivered* to date — same
   discipline as any close (see [tap-me-out.md](tap-me-out.md)). This is the
   deliverable; it's done, not abandoned.
2. **Create a "Part 2" ticket** for what remains. Carry over only the
   *outstanding* scope — not the whole original ask. Link it back to Part 1.
3. **Send Part 2 through planning.** It is a *new* candidate, not an automatic
   continuation. Planning decides whether the remaining work is still worth
   doing, at what priority, against everything else. The answer might be "no".

## Why re-plan instead of just continuing

The original ticket was prioritised with old information. By the time it's
overrun, the remaining work competes with everything learned since. Routing
Part 2 through planning forces that comparison instead of granting the leftover
scope automatic right-of-way. Sunk cost is not a reason to finish.

## Signals it's time to split

- Crossed a sprint boundary still open (strongest signal).
- Original estimate blown and the finish line keeps moving.
- Scope has quietly grown past what was agreed.
- Genuinely separable delivered work sits inside an unfinished whole.
